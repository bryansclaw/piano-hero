# Practice Guide

## Getting Started with Practice Mode

### 1. Select a Song
Navigate to the **🏠 Home** tab and click any song to load it. Then switch to **📖 Practice** to access practice tools.

### 2. Practice Tools Overview

#### Tempo Control (25% - 150%)
- Start slow (50-75%) when learning a new piece
- The slider adjusts playback speed without changing pitch
- As you gain confidence, gradually increase toward 100%

#### Section Looping
- Enable looping and set start/end measures
- The selected section repeats infinitely until you disable it
- Focus on small sections (4-8 measures) for targeted practice

#### Metronome
- Toggle the metronome for a steady click track
- The click syncs to the song's BPM, adjusted for your tempo setting
- Essential for developing rhythmic accuracy

#### Count-In
- When enabled, gives you a 1-2-3-4 count before the song starts
- Helps you prepare mentally and find the tempo

#### Auto Speed-Up Mode
- Start at a comfortable slow tempo
- After each successful loop, tempo increases by 5%
- Set your target tempo (e.g., 100%)
- The mode stops increasing when you reach the target

### 3. Recommended Practice Workflow

1. **Listen first**: Play the song at 100% without trying to play along
2. **Slow it down**: Set tempo to 50% and start with the right hand only (easy difficulty)
3. **Loop trouble spots**: Identify sections you struggle with and loop them
4. **Use metronome**: Enable the metronome for rhythmic stability
5. **Speed up gradually**: Use auto speed-up or manually increase by 10-15%
6. **Add the left hand**: Switch to medium difficulty for both hands
7. **Record yourself**: Use the recording feature to track progress
8. **Review AI feedback**: Check the post-session analysis for specific insights

## Curriculum Guide

### Skill Paths

#### 🎵 Fundamentals
**Best for**: Complete beginners
- Covers hand position, note reading, basic scales
- Start here if you've never played piano
- 10 lessons from Middle C to simple melodies

#### 🎶 Chords & Harmony
**Best for**: Players who know basic notes
- Major/minor triads, inversions, 7th chords
- Common chord progressions (I-IV-V-I, I-V-vi-IV)
- Essential for understanding song structure

#### 👁️ Sight Reading
**Best for**: Improving note recognition speed
- Progressive difficulty reading drills
- Both treble and bass clef
- Mix of rhythmic values and accidentals

#### ✋ Technique
**Best for**: Building finger strength and independence
- Legato/staccato, arpeggios, octaves
- Dynamic control (piano, forte, crescendo)
- Hand independence exercises

#### ⭐ Song Mastery
**Best for**: Applying skills to real music
- Curated passages from the Taylor Swift library
- Progresses from easy intros to complex bridges
- Each lesson focuses on a specific song section

### Daily Practice Plans

The **📋 Practice Plan** button generates personalized plans:

- **15 minutes**: Quick warm-up + 1 lesson or weak spot
- **30 minutes**: Warm-up + weak spots + 2 lessons + song practice
- **60 minutes**: Full session with warm-up, technique, lessons, and free play

Plans adapt based on your analytics data:
- Prioritizes weak keys identified by the heatmap
- Suggests next available lessons in your curriculum
- Recommends songs you haven't mastered yet

## Recording & Playback

### Recording a Session
1. Navigate to **📖 Practice**
2. Click the **🎙️ Record** button
3. Play your piece
4. Click **Stop** when finished
5. The recording is automatically saved to your library

### Managing Recordings
- **▶ Play**: Replay the recording with visual note highlighting
- **📝 Note**: Add journal notes ("Finally nailed the bridge!")
- **📋 Copy**: Copy a text summary to clipboard for sharing
- **🗑️ Delete**: Remove recordings you no longer need

### Practice Journal Tips
- Note what you worked on: "Focused on left hand measures 12-16"
- Record your mood: "Feeling frustrated with the chorus"
- Track breakthroughs: "Finally played the bridge at 100% speed!"
- Set goals: "Next session: try this at 90% tempo"

## Using AI Feedback

After each game session, the AI analyzer provides:

### Understanding Your Grade
- **S (98%+)**: Near-perfect — focus on expression and dynamics
- **A (90%+)**: Excellent — minor timing tweaks needed
- **B (80%+)**: Good — specific sections need attention
- **C (70%+)**: Decent — use practice tools to improve weak spots
- **D (60%+)**: Needs work — slow down and loop difficult sections
- **F (<60%)**: Keep at it — drop the difficulty or tempo

### Acting on Timing Insights
- **"You rush the left hand"**: Practice left hand alone at slower tempo
- **"Measures 12-16 are late"**: Loop those measures, focus on anticipation
- **"Low steadiness"**: Use metronome to develop consistent timing

### Acting on Dynamics Insights
- **"Too uniform"**: Consciously vary your touch — accent downbeats
- **"Playing too softly"**: Focus on pressing with confidence, not force
- **"Wide velocity range"**: Good if intentional; work on control if not

## Analytics Dashboard

### Reading the Practice Time Chart
- Shows daily minutes practiced over the selected period
- Aim for consistency over volume — 15 min daily > 2 hours once a week

### Reading the Accuracy Trend
- Select a song to see how your accuracy improves over time
- Expect non-linear progress — plateaus are normal
- Celebrate upward trends, don't stress small dips

### Key Accuracy Heatmap
- Green keys = high accuracy (80%+)
- Yellow keys = moderate accuracy (50-80%)
- Red keys = low accuracy (<50%)
- Focus practice on red keys using technique exercises
